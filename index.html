Overview

What this design is trying to solve

Movement-first layout

The key map is organized according to how the hands actually move, with zones reflecting thumb, middle, ring, and pinky roles instead of forcing legacy rectangular habits into AR space.

Built for spatial input

The concept is intended for AR keyboards, virtual input surfaces, and other environments where geometry, hand posture, and reach are more important than copying a standard laptop keyboard.

Original presentation

This page exists to publicly present the visual layout, the mapping logic, and the claim of authorship for the supplied design assets.

Design logic

Mapped around hand zones and finger behavior

The layout shown here groups keys by expected hand position and movement pathways. The result is a more organic arrangement for immersive typing, especially where a flat rectangular board is not the natural interface.

Central access for high-frequency keys and shared reach.

Peripheral placement for keys better suited to outer-finger travel.

Thumb and lower-zone positioning for modifier and control interaction.

Curved geometry that matches a more natural hand envelope.
